# All ARM MacBooks — Analysis & Buyer Philosophy

Data sourced from `all arm macbooks.csv` — 548 configurations spanning November 2020 through March 2026.

---

## The Used Market Advantage

One of the best things about ARM MacBooks is that there is no performance loss with a used machine. The M chip architecture doesn't degrade the way Intel chips did. A used M1 Pro from 2021 runs the same as it did on day one. Combined with how many configurations Apple has released, the used market is extremely accessible — there's a MacBook for every budget if you know what to look for.

---

## What to Actually Look For

### 1. Ports — The Line in the Sand

The M1 Pro 14" (October 2021) introduced the port design that matters:

- MagSafe charging
- HDMI
- SD card slot
- 3x USB-C (Thunderbolt)

This is peak MacBook design and has been the standard ever since. Apple eventually brought this same layout to the base MacBook Pro. Anything before October 2021 — the M1 Air, M1 Pro 13" — uses the old dongle-era port setup. Start your search here.

### 2. RAM

The most important spec. You can't upgrade it after purchase. Buy as much as you can afford.

### 3. CPU / GPU

Second priority after RAM. More on chip generations below.

### 4. Form Factor Rule

- **Pro chip → get the 14"**
- **Max chip → get the 16"**

The 16" costs $200–300 more for the same chip and config. For a Pro chip, that premium isn't worth it. For a Max chip, the larger display and thermal headroom justify it.

### 5. Storage

Storage doesn't matter as much as people think — iCloud and external SSDs solve this problem cheaply. That said, **2TB is the practical ceiling**. Going to 4TB or 8TB adds $800–1,200+ to the price and signals you haven't optimized your workflow. 512GB–2TB is the sweet spot.

## Chip Generations — The Real Story

| Generation | Year | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| M1 | 2020–2021 | The great enlightenment. Apple Silicon's arrival changed everything. |
| M2 | 2022 | Stepping stone. M1 refined, not reimagined. |
| M3 | 2023 | Stepping stone. 3nm process, real-world gains were modest. |
| M4 | 2024 | The beast. Perfected the architecture. Best value proposition. |
| M5 | 2025–2026 | Latest and greatest. Tests being done every day. |

M1 was the paradigm shift. M2 and M3 were iterations. M4 is where the tech matured. M5 builds on that foundation with the newest silicon.

## Current Sweet Spots in the Lineup

### Budget — Used M1 Pro 14"
The machine that started the great design: MagSafe, HDMI, SD slot, 3x USB-C. Buy used. Still an exceptional machine. 16GB or 32GB RAM, 512GB–1TB storage.

### Mid-Range — M4 Pro 14"
The architecture fully matured. Pro chip in the right form factor. 24GB or 48GB RAM. Don't go past 2TB storage.

### High-End — M4 Max 16" or M5 Max 16"
Max chip belongs in the 16". 36GB–128GB RAM. 1TB–2TB storage.

### The Base MacBook Pro 14" (M4/M5)
Worth knowing: the 14" chassis with base M4/M5 (no Pro chip) exists in the $1,599–$3,199 range and shares the same port layout. A solid option if you don't need Pro-level performance but want the full port design.

### Notable: MacBook Neo
Launched March 2026, the MacBook Neo uses an **A18 Pro** chip (iPhone silicon) — not M-series. Two configs at $599 and $699 with 8GB RAM. Apple's first sub-$700 Mac laptop, a budget tier that didn't exist before.

### RAM Ceiling (Max chips)
128GB has been the ceiling since M3. M4 and M5 Max didn't raise it.

### Most vs. Least Expensive ARM Mac
- **Cheapest**: MacBook Neo 13" — $599
- **Most expensive**: MacBook Pro 16" M5 Max 128GB/8TB — $7,349 (12.3x difference)
